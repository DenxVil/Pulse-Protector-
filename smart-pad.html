Innovation

Cuffless Blood Pressure via PTT

Non-invasive blood pressure estimation using Pulse Transit Time

Pulse Transit Time (PTT) is the time delay between the ECG R-peak (electrical signal) and the arrival of the PPG pulse wave (blood flow). Since blood pressure affects how fast blood travels through arteries, PTT has an inverse relationship with BP - higher pressure means faster pulse wave velocity.

üìä

ECG R-Peak

Detected by AD8232 module, marks the electrical trigger of heart contraction

üíì

PPG Peak

Detected by MAX30102, marks when blood pulse arrives at the sensor

PTT Calculation

Time difference between R-peak and PPG peak in milliseconds

// Pulse Transit Time calculation PTT = Time_of_PPG_peak - Time_of_ECG_R_peak // Blood Pressure estimation (inverse relationship) SBP = a / PTT + b // Systolic Blood Pressure DBP = c / PTT + d // Diastolic Blood Pressure // Typical calibrated formula: // SBP ‚âà 120 - k √ó (PTT - PTT_baseline) // Where k ‚âà 0.5-1.5 depending on calibration

Calibration Required

PTT-based BP requires initial calibration against a reference cuff measurement. After calibration, accuracy of ¬±5-10 mmHg is achievable. Recalibration is recommended periodically or when physiological changes occur.

Placement

Smart Pad Body Placement Guide

Optimal placement for accurate vital sign monitoring

üìç

Left Upper Chest

Place the Smart Pad below the left clavicle, over the pectoral area. This location provides optimal signal acquisition for all sensors.

Why This Location?
  • Close proximity to heart for optimal ECG signal
  • Good chest vasculature for PPG readings
  • Skin surface temperature near core body temp
  • Stable placement with minimal motion artifacts

üìã Placement Instructions

1. Clean the Area

Wipe the skin with alcohol pad to remove oils and ensure good electrode contact.

2. Position the Pad

Place 2-3 inches below the left collarbone, centered over the pectoral muscle.

3. Secure with Adhesive

Ensure the pad is firmly adhered with no gaps. Use the included medical adhesive strips.

4. Attach ECG Electrodes

Place RA (right arm) electrode on right shoulder, LA (left arm) on left shoulder, RL (right leg) reference on lower right chest.

5. Verify Connection

Check the LED indicator for successful BLE connection and valid vital sign readings.
